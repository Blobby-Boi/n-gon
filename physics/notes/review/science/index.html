Hypothesis, Theory, and Law

In science the words hypothesis, theory, and law have special meanings that don't exactly agree with how they are used outside of science.

A hypothesis is a proposed explanation for a phenomenon. A hypothesis is an educated guess about something. Often scientists test a hypothesis through experimentation.

A scientific theory is a well-confirmed explanation of a phenomenon. Theories have been rigorously tested many times. The word theory outside of science is used for guesses, but in science it is the highest level of knowledge.

A scientific law is a similar to a theory, but with some limitations. They don't offer any explanation, just a relationship. Additionally laws generally only apply to a limit situation. Laws are often conclusions drawn from some initial postulates.

Modeling of Reality

Science builds models to simplified a narrow aspect of reality. These models are often very accurate as long as they stay within a range of values. For example newtonian physics works very well, but it becomes inaccurate near the sped of light. We can switch to relativistic physics to get accurate predictions near the speed of light.

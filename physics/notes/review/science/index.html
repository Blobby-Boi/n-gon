Hypothesis, Theory, and Law

In science the words hypothesis, law, and, theory have special meanings that don't exactly agree with how they are used outside of science.


A scientific law summarizes the results of experiments or observations.

  • They don't offer any explanation, just a relationship.
  • Laws often only apply to a model, not directly to the real world.
  • Scientific laws are practical conclusions reached by the scientific method.

    • Examples: Ohm's Law, Universal Gravitation, Coulomb's law, Kirchhoff's laws


    A hypothesis is a proposed explanation for a phenomenon.

  • A hypothesis is an educated guess.
  • Scientists test hypotheses through experimentation.

    • Examples: , , ,


    A scientific theory is a well-confirmed explanation of a phenomenon.

  • Theories have been rigorously tested many times.
  • The word theory outside of science is used for guesses, but in science it is the highest level of knowledge.

    • Examples: big bang theory, quantum field theory, evolution, germ theory

    Modeling of Reality

    Science builds models to explain an aspect of reality. Most models are only accurate within a range of conditions.

    Newtonian physics is a model that explains how things move, but it isn't a perfect description of reality. Relativistic physics is a more accurate model of reality near the speed of light, and quantum mechanics is more accurate for very short distances. No current scientific model can describe our universe perfectly.
